⚠ Critical Gap
Only 23.1% (9/39) of schools meet all WASH standards simultaneously. Sanitation is the primary bottleneck — non-functional toilets affect 51.3% of schools.
📌 Urban vs Rural
Urban schools show 78% functional water access vs 47% in rural counterparts. Rural schools also show 34% lower handwash compliance, suggesting systemic resource disparity.
✓ Positive Indicator
Schools with dedicated WASH champions show 2.4× higher handwash compliance rates. The WASH champion programme model shows strong promise for replication.

🔴 Critical — Water Testing
Only 33.3% of schools conduct regular water quality testing. Of the 26 untested schools, 11 are in fluoride-risk zones (Aurangabad & Beed districts).
⚡ Seasonal Disruption
68% of rural schools report water supply disruptions during summer months (April–June), forcing students to use unprotected sources for up to 8 weeks.
📊 Girls' Access
Separate water points for girls — as per Swachh Bharat Mission guidelines — present in only 48.7% of schools. This gap disproportionately affects girls' WASH experience.

🚨 Functionality Gap
82.1% of schools have toilets, but only 48.7% have functional ones — a 33.4 percentage-point functionality gap. The majority of non-functional toilets require plumbing or structural repair.
👧 Menstrual Hygiene
Only 43.6% of schools have MHM-compliant facilities (private toilets with disposal units). This is a significant barrier to school attendance for adolescent girls.
📐 Ratio Concern
Average toilet-to-pupil ratio is 1:42 — significantly above the recommended 1:25. Ashram (residential) schools show the most critical shortfall at 1:68.

🏫 Study Population
  • 39 schools across 6 Maharashtra districts
  • School types: ZP, Municipal, Private, Ashram
  • Enrolment range: 68–842 students
  • 6 districts: Pune, Nashik, Aurangabad, Beed, Solapur, Kolhapur
  • Purposive sampling — maximum variation approach
🔧 Data Collection Methods
  • Structured WASH observation checklist (WHO/UNICEF JMP)
  • Direct observation by trained field teams
  • Key informant interviews: headteacher + WASH staff
  • Student focus group discussions (n = 78 students)
  • Photographic documentation (geo-tagged)
📐 Assessment Framework
  • WHO/UNICEF Joint Monitoring Programme (JMP) WASH indicators
  • India's Swachh Bharat Mission school standards
  • MDG/SDG 6 (Clean Water & Sanitation) targets
  • Composite WASH score: weighted sum of 18 indicators
  • Hygiene observation protocol: WHO Five Moments
📊 Data Pipeline
  • Field data: KoBoCollect / ODK mobile forms
  • Cleaned in Microsoft Excel (XLOOKUP, Power Query)
  • Analysis in Python (Pandas, NumPy, Matplotlib)
  • WASH score computation: custom weighted formula
  • Outputs: xlsx, HTML dashboard, PDF report
🌍 Policy Context
  • Swachh Bharat Mission (Gramin) Phase II (2020–2025)
  • National School Sanitation Programme guidelines
  • SDG 6: Clean Water & Sanitation by 2030
  • UNICEF WASH in Schools Global Framework
  • Maharashtra State Education Dept WASH norms
⚠️ Limitations
  • Cross-sectional snapshot — seasonal variation not captured
  • Self-report bias in teacher/headteacher interviews
  • 39-school sample limits statistical power for subgroups
  • Cost estimates are approximations from district PWD schedules
  • Data collected pre-monsoon (Feb 2025) — water scarcity may understate wet-season access
